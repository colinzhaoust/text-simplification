Judge the quality of similar sentences

Please read the groups of sentences below. Each group contains an original sentence along with some variations of it. Your job is to grade the quality of the variations with two 4-point scales: meaning and grammar. 

Meaning

  • 4 - identical: All of the meaning of the original sentence is retained, and nothing is added or missing
  • 3 - minor differences: The meaning of the original sentence is retained, although some minor information may be altered, deleted or added without too great a difference in meaning
  • 2 - moderate differences: Some of the meaning of the original sentence is retained, although a non-trivial amount of information was deleted or added
  • 1 - substantially different: Substantial amount of the meaning is different

Grammar

  • 4 - grammatical (but can be awkward): The sentence is grammatical, but might sound slightly awkward
  • 3 - one or two errors: The sentence has one or two error (such as an agreement error between subject and verb, a plural noun with a singular determiner, or the wrong verb form)
  • 2 - many errors: The sentence has multiple errors or omits words that would be required to make it grammatical
  • 1 - ungrammatical: The sentence is totally ungrammatical

Here is an example of how we graded a pair of sentences:

Original sentence:
they are culturally akin to the coastal peoples of papua new guinea .
Variation: Meaning Grammar
it 's culture similar to the people of port of papua . 3 4

We gave this variation a "3" for grammar because it is almost grammatical but uses word "its" instead "it's" by mistake. We gave it a "2" for meaning because some of the meaning of the original sentence is still there but a lot of the meaning is missing.
