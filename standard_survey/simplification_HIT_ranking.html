Instructions

For this task you are given one source sentence and four (4) simplifications of the sentence. Your job is to rank the simplifications on how well they simplify the wording of the original sentence.

  • A good simplified sentence should use words, phrases and grammatical structures that are easy to understand and very straight forward. Think of it as a sentence written for a non-native speaker or student.
    Example:
    Original : They are culturally akin to the coastal people.
    Simplified: Their culture is similar to the people of the coast.
     
  • You should also consider grammar when ranking sentences. A sentence that does not make sense will be worse than one that is not completely simplified.
     
  • Better simplifications are ones that preserve as much as the original meaning as possible and don't omit details.
    Example:
    Original : It was originally thought that the debris thrown up by the collision filled in the smaller craters .
    Good : Originally people thought that the small craters were filled in by the pieces thrown up by the crash.
    Bad : The craters were filled by thrown up dirt.
    (Notice that in the bad example, there is significant information loss, "originally", "thought","smaller" and "collision" are not reflected at all)
     
  • If two sentences are the same or very similar (wording or complexity), you may mark them as a tie by giving them the same ranking. Please try your best to determine the ranking and only mark sentences as a tie when you really feel like you need to.
    Example:
    Simplification A: He went to the convention on January 13th, 1988
    Simplification B: He went to the convention on January 13-th, 1988

     
  • Only relative ranking matters outside of a tie. E.g. Ranking sentences as [1 1 3 4] is the same as ranking them [1 1 2 3] or [1 1 2 4]
     

This task will be used to evaluate the results of human and machine simplifications by gathering multiple rankings. A sample of your results will be manually checked for accuracy among your peers so please take the time to understand the instructions and do the task carefully.

Have fun and Thank you!
